How I Came To Love Computers

My interest in cyberspace/computing first began with video games, most notably Megaman. Megaman Battle Network 3 for the Gameboy Advanced was more focused on networks rather than robots. You had a PET (Personal Terminal) which contained a customizable artificial intelligence, know as a NetNavi. MegaMan.exe, who was your NetNavi, helped you surf the internet and battle against viruses. The NetNavi protected itself using defensive programming already built into them but as the Navi operator you also had the ability to send weapon programs from the PET via BattleChips.

When I was 13 years old I attended Emagination Computer Camps (yea, they exist). To be honest, I was beyond excited. Even though I only attended for 2 weeks, I did my best to get exposed to all the cool technologies that were available. I dormed at the camp and had a new learning experience every single day. Later on in life, when i was in my freshman year of college, I decided to work at Emagination Computer Camps. I taught robotics and PBASIC, which is a microcontroller-based version of Basic.

(I am in the center)

I attended the 2018 JPMorgan Chase Code for Good Challenge in Delaware. This was one of the first hackathons that I had ever attended. My team and I came up with a technology solution for GoodWill. This was a 24 hour challenge and I enjoyed my time there very much. Even though I was not as technologically experienced as some of my teammates, they were all willing to teach me new tools and technologies. Because of this experience, everyday I have a drive to learn new things and become even better at what I already know.
